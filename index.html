Methodology & data provenance
How the score is built

The headline number is a composite 0–100 correction-risk score: a weighted sum of six signal blocks. Each block is scored 0–100 by how many of its sub-signals breach defined thresholds and by how far. The formula is:

Score = 0.25·Valuation + 0.25·Capex/Revenue + 0.20·Credit + 0.15·Demand + 0.10·Technical + 0.05·News

Color bands: 🟢 0–34 low · 🟡 35–64 rising · 🔴 65–100 high. The trajectory matters as much as the level: a one-day jump of +10 or three consecutive rising days is flagged as a warning even below the red band.

Confidence & limitations (read this before sharing)
  • Confidence = Medium today. Confidence reflects how many signals had fresh, directly-sourced data. It is not a measure of accuracy.
  • Proxies are disclosed. The Bloomberg US 20 AI Select (B100QAI) P/E was not directly accessible, so the Nasdaq-100 P/E (~36) is used as a proxy for the AI complex. VIX intraday level was n/a at run time.
  • This is a heuristic, not a forecast. Weights and thresholds are judgment-based, not statistically estimated. The score expresses relative pressure, not a probability or a price target.
  • No look-ahead, no leverage advice. The monitor does not recommend positions, sizing, or timing.
  • Data freshness. Figures are point-in-time as of the date shown; markets move intraday.
